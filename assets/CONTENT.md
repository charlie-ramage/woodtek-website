## Existing FAQ copy (verbatim — can use)

### What is Cross Laminated Timber?
Cross Laminated Timber (CLT) is made by stacking and pressing layers of dried wood together. The layers are bonded with adhesives. Each new layer is placed perpendicular to the previous one, which makes the panels much stronger than conventional wood structures.

### What distance can CLT span between structural elements?
This depends what thickness of panel you choose to go with and how many bearers the panel will span across. Each factory supplying CLT has documents available for design engineers to calculate spans and cantilever distances.

### How does Mass timber achieve code regulations for fire resistance?
When Mass timber is exposed to fire, it chars on the outside forming an insulated layer which protects the interior wood from damage. This allows it to achieve a fire rating higher than code requirements. One or two layers of GIB Fyreline plasterboard can be added to extend the fire resistance rating (FRR) for longer protection of the timber element.

### How long will it take to install?
Elements go together quickly like Lego — this is dependent on site accessibility, the weather, and the main contractor's programme.

### How does it compare environmentally against concrete / steel structures?
Using timber lowers a building's environmental impact and carbon footprint, because trees sequester carbon during their lifecycle.

### What fixings hold the timber elements together?
Screws installed at specified centres designed by the structural engineer. After installation Woodtek provides QA documents with photos to the main contractor for assurance.
